# Slide 11 of Lecture 8 contains information about Danish eCommerce Buyer Behavior


# The top section of the diagram in slide 11 of lecture 8 presents aggregate European e-commerce metrics through a horizontal bar chart. Internet penetration reaches 99 percent across the European average. The percentage of e-commerce consumers stands at 88 percent of the population. Of these e-commerce consumers, 71 percent have shopped from abroad, indicating significant cross-border online shopping activity. The average purchase per month amounts to EUR 243. The percentage who shopped online more often due to coronavirus represents 28 percent of consumers, showing the pandemic's impact on online shopping behavior.

The bottom left chart displays the percentage of e-commerce consumers who have made purchases from various product categories over the past year. Clothing and footwear leads as the most popular category at 52 percent. Home electronics follows at 33 percent. Cosmetics and skin care products account for 27 percent of purchases. Pharmacy products represent 26 percent. Groceries show 23 percent adoption in online purchasing. Dietary supplements and Books and media both register at 21 percent. Animal products represent the smallest category shown at 18 percent.

The bottom right chart illustrates shopping frequency across major e-commerce platforms, with the basis being consumers who have shopped online in the past year. Zalando demonstrates the highest usage at 42.2 percent among those who have shopped online. eBay shows 22 percent penetration among online shoppers. Amazon registers at 20 percent. The bars are displayed with a light blue fill extending to 100 percent, with the actual percentage values highlighted in darker blue at the left end of each bar.

The data is sourced from PostNord's eCommerce in Europe report, with additional detailed data available at the provided URL reference.
