# Slide 13 of Lecture 4 contains information about Just to notice! “Operations” Management. Some More Milestones in History
The timeline presents contributions to Operations Management since 1900, organized chronologically with the year, contribution description, and associated contributor names.

Beginning in 1900, Scientific Management, Functional Organization, and Cost Systems were introduced by Frederick Taylor. In the same year, Motion Study was developed by Frank Gilbreth.

In 1901, Gantt Charts for Scheduling and Monitoring were created by Henry Gantt. Moving to 1910, Efficiency Principles were established by Harrington Emerson.

The year 1913 marked the introduction of the Moving Assembly Line by Henry Ford. In 1914 and 1933, Statistical Tables and Techniques were developed by Karl Pearson.

In 1915, the Economic Order Quantity Concept for Inventory Management was introduced by F. W. Harris.

The timeline then shifts to the right column, starting with 1924, when Statistical Techniques for Quality Control were developed by H. F. Dodge, G. H. Romig, and W. A. Shewhart.

In 1934, Activity Sampling Techniques were created by L. H. C. Tippett. The year 1938 saw the emergence of Operations Research, attributed to P.M.X. Blackett and Others.

In 1947, Applications of Linear Programming were introduced by G. B. Dantzig, followed in 1949 by Systems Analysis developed by Norbert Weiner and Jay Forrester.

The period since 1950 encompasses numerous developments with Computer Applications including Simulation, Materials Requirements Planning (MRP), Computer Aided Design and Manufacturing (CAD/CAM), Decision Theory, Numerically Controlled Machines, and Robotics, credited to Numerous Contributors.
