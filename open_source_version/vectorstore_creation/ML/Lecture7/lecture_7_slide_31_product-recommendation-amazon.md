In a normalized user-item matrix, Product 1 is most similar to Product 3 (bought by many people), Product 2 is most like Product 4 (also bought by John and Yoko), and Product 3 is most similar to Product 1, showing that normalization causes the system to prefer popular items when making recommendations.
