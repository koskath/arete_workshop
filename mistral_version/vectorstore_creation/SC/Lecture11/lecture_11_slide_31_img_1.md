# The image 1 in slide 31 of lecture 11 contains:
The image contains a two-column list of directives, each followed by an explanation. Each directive is bolded, and the text is left-aligned.

Left Column:
1. Discuss: Consider and debate or argue about the pros and cons of an issue. Write about any conflict. Compare and contrast.
2. Enumerate: List several ideas, aspects, events, things, qualities, reasons, etc.
3. Evaluate: Give your opinion or cite the opinion of an expert. Include evidence to support the evaluation.
4. Explain: Make an idea clear. Show logically how a concept is developed. Give the reason for an event.
5. Illustrate: Give concrete examples. Explain clearly by using comparisons or examples.
6. Interpret: Comment upon, give examples, describe relationships. Explain the meaning. Describe, then evaluate.
7. Outline: Describe main ideas, characteristics, or events. (Does not necessarily mean to write a Roman Numeral/Letter outline.)
8. Prove: Support with facts (especially facts presented in class or in the text).
9. State: Explain precisely.

Right Column:
1. Relate: Show the connections between ideas or events. Provide a larger context.
2. Summarize: Give a brief, condensed account. Include conclusions. Avoid unnecessary details.
3. Trace: Show the order of events or progress of a subject or event.
4. Define: Give the meaning; usually a meaning specific to the course or subject. Explain the exact meaning. Definitions are usually short.
5. Describe: Give a detailed account. Make a picture with words. List characteristics, qualities, and parts.
6. Analyze: Break into separate parts and discuss, examine, or interpret each part.
7. Contrast: Show differences. Set in opposition.
8. Compare: Examine two or more things. Identify similarities and differences.
9. Criticize: Make judgments. Evaluate comparative worth. Criticism often involves analysis.
