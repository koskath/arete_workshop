1. Longer Chains including the "Last Mile"

The Amazon Dash Buttons (top left) and Amazon Lockers (top right) illustrate how supply chains now extend directly into consumers' homes and neighborhoods
The "last mile" - getting products from a distribution center to the final customer's door - has become one of the most challenging and expensive parts of the supply chain
E-commerce has dramatically increased the complexity of this final delivery stage

This reflects the shift toward sustainability and the circular economy, where logistics must manage not just forward flows but also the return, recycling, and reuse of materials and products.
