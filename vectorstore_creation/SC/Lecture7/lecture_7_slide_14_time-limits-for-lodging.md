# Description of the Image: Time Limits for Lodging Pre-Declarations (ENS, EXS)

The image presents a table explaining the required time limits for submitting **ENS (Entry Summary Declaration)** and **EXS (Exit Summary Declaration)** based on different modes of transport. These declarations are part of EU customs safety and security procedures.

## Table Structure

The table contains **three columns**:

1. **Mode of Transport**  
   Specifies the type of transport used (e.g., maritime cargo, air transportation, rail, road).

2. **ENS**  
   Indicates the deadline for submitting the entry summary declaration **before arrival** (or before loading, depending on transport type).

3. **EXS**  
   Indicates the deadline for submitting the exit summary declaration **before departure** (or before loading).

---

## Time Limits by Mode of Transport

### Maritime Cargo (containerized)
- **ENS:** 24 hours before the container is loaded onto the vessel  
- **EXS:** 24 hours before the container is loaded onto the vessel  

### Maritime Cargo (non-containerized)
- **ENS:** 4 hours before arrival at the first port in the customs territory  
- **EXS:** 4 hours before departure  

### Short Sea Shipping
- **ENS:** 2 hours before arrival  
- **EXS:** 2 hours before departure  

### Air Transportation (long-haul flights)
- **ENS:** 4 hours before arrival  
- **EXS:** 0.5 hours before departure  

### Air Transportation (short-haul flights)
- **ENS:** 2 hours before arrival  
- **EXS:** 0.5 hours before departure  

### Rail Transportation
- **ENS:** 2 hours before arrival  
- **EXS:** 2 hours before departure  

### Inland Waterway Navigation
- **ENS:** 2 hours before arrival  
- **EXS:** 2 hours before departure  

### Road Transportation
- **ENS:** 1 hour before arrival  
- **EXS:** 1 hour before departure  

---

# Overall Purpose
The table summarizes the **regulatory deadlines** for safety and security declarations required when goods enter or leave the EU. These deadlines vary depending on the speed and type of transport used.
