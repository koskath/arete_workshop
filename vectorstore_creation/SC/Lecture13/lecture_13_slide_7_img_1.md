# The image 1 in slide 7 of lecture 13 contains:
The image presents a strategic hierarchy framework divided into three levels: Corporate strategy, Business strategy, and Functional strategy.

1. **Corporate Strategy:**
   - **Key Strategic Decisions:** Focuses on broad organizational issues like deciding which business areas to engage in, potential acquisitions, divestitures, and cash allocation.
   - **Influences on Decision-Making:** External factors such as the economic, social, and political environments, along with the company’s values and ethics, guide decision-making at this level.

2. **Business Strategy:**
   - **Key Strategic Decisions:** Involves defining the mission, setting strategic objectives, and determining how to compete in the market.
   - **Influences on Decision-Making:** Influenced by customer/market dynamics, competitor activities, core technology dynamics, and financial constraints.

3. **Functional Strategy:**
   - **Key Strategic Decisions:** Concerns how to contribute to strategic objectives and manage function-specific resources.
   - **Influences on Decision-Making:** Determined by the skills of the function’s staff, current technology, and the function’s recent performance.

**Connection to Supply Chain Analytics:**
- **Corporate Strategy:** Supply chain analytics can inform major strategic decisions like acquisitions or divestitures by providing data-driven insights on market trends and financial performance.
- **Business Strategy:** Analytics help in understanding customer behavior, optimizing supply chain processes, and competitive benchmarking to enhance market positioning.
- **Functional Strategy:** Analytics are crucial for resource management, improving operational efficiency, and developing technological capabilities within the supply chain.
