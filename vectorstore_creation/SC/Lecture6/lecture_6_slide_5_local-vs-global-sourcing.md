# Slide 5 of Lecture 6 contains information about Local vs. Global Sourcing

Local sourcing refers to the sourcing of food, ingredients and other consumable products from within a specific radius or distance from where they will be used or sourced, or from a given geographical area. In contrast, global sourcing is the practice of sourcing from the global market for goods and services across geopolitical boundaries, which often aims to exploit global efficiencies in the delivery of a product or service. 5
