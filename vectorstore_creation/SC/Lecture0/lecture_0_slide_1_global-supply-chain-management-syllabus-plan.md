# Slide 1 of Lecture 0 contains information about Global Supply Chain Management Syllabus Plan


Faculty: Günter Prockl (GP) - course coordinator
Hans-Joachim Schramm (HJS)
Time & room: Check schedule & info on Learn/E-Campus
Examination: 4-hour (closed book) written exam
Note: The faculty reserves the right to changes.

The following provides the Date, Session, Literature and Faculty information in continuous text instead of a table. 

On 5th February, the session “1. Introduction to Logistics and Supply Chain Management” is offered. For this session, the main Literature is the Textbook: Management Chapter 1 and the Reading List: No. 1, 2, and the Faculty is Günter Prockl (GP). The content of this session includes an introduction to the course, the evolution of logistics and SCM, key logistics activities and components, and the grand purpose of supply chain management.

On 7th February, the session “2. The Global Context of Logistics and SCM” takes place. The Literature for this session consists of the Textbook: Chapter 2, 7 and the Reading List: No. 3, 4, 5, and the Faculty responsible is again Günter Prockl (GP). This session covers globalisation, containerization, international trade and logistics, the logistics/SCM of cross-border product movement, the global environment of logistics and SCM, and global logistics and SCM applications.

On 12th February, the session “3. Integration, Collaboration and SCM” is scheduled. The Literature for this session is Textbook: Chapter 5 (12) together with the Reading List: No. 6, 7, 8, 9, and the Faculty member in charge is Günter Prockl (GP). This session focuses on organisation, coordination and integration issues in the global supply chain, intra/inter-organisational relationships, supply chain collaboration methods and initiatives, supply chain complexity and connectivity, and stakeholder Management.

On 14th February, the session “4. Global Production in the Supply Chain” is taught by Günter Prockl (GP). The core Literature is Textbook: Chapter 3 together with the Reading List: No. 10, 11, 12, 13. This session addresses milestones in the history of production, the main production systems – Mass, Lean and Agile, Push-Pull-Strategies, Postponement, and Global Production Networks.

On 17th February, the session “5. Inventory Management, Planning and Control” is offered. The Literature consists of Textbook: Chapter 10 and the Reading List: No. 14, 15, and the Faculty is Hans-Joachim Schramm (HJS). In this session, the topics include ABC/XYZ classification, EOQ and other inventory management concepts, inventory control systems and approaches, and supply chain inventory management.

On 19th February, the session “6. Outsourcing, Offshoring and Procurement in the Supply Chain” is conducted by Hans-Joachim Schramm (HJS). The Literature is Textbook: Chapter 12 and Reading List: No. 16, 17, 18, 19. The content covers outsourcing, global sourcing and offshoring, supplier selection and the procurement process, buyer-supplier relationships, and Total Cost of Ownership and unit total costs.

On 20th February, the session “7. Management of International Sales and Goods Flows” takes place. The Literature includes Textbook: Chapter 9 and the Reading List: No. 20, 21, and the Faculty is Hans-Joachim Schramm (HJS). This session focuses on economic integration levels and trade facilitation, Lex Mercatoria and Trade Terms, especially Incoterms® 2020, and documents required in international trade flows.

On 24th February, the session “8. Distribution Systems and Service Supply Chains” is given by Hans-Joachim Schramm (HJS). The Literature for this session is Textbook: Chapter 4, 15 and Reading List: No. 22, 23, 24. The topics comprise Distribution Systems and Service Supply Chains, Distribution Channels – Omni Channels, online vs. brick-and-mortar retailing, Service Supply Chains, Servitisation, and Reverse Logistics Systems.

On 26th February, the session “9. Materials Handling and Warehousing” is taught by Hans-Joachim Schramm (HJS). The Literature for this session is Textbook: Chapter 11 and Reading List: No. 25, 26, 27. The topics include warehouse layout and warehousing concepts, cross docking, materials planning and control, and logistics performance: inventory turnover, supply chain ratio and cash-to-cash-cycle.

On 28th February, the session “10. Transport and Logistics Services in Supply Chains” is offered. It is taught by Hans-Joachim Schramm (HJS), and the Literature consists of Textbook: Chapter 6, 8 and Reading List: No. 28, 29, 30. This session covers transport mode characteristics and selection, transport and logistics service providers, and management of transport and logistics operations.

On 12th March, the “11. Mid-Term workshop: Exam preparation” is conducted by Günter Prockl (GP).

On 14th March, the session “12. Digitization and Digitalization in the Supply Chain” is scheduled. The Literature is Textbook: Chapter 13 together with the Reading List: No. 31, 32, 33, and the Faculty is Günter Prockl (GP). This session discusses legacy systems: MRP, ERP, CPFR, VMI, SCEM, visibility, transparency, and data analytics, digital transformation in transport and logistics, and Supply Chain vs. Ecosystem.

On 19th March, the session “13. Supply chain strategies and configurations for globalisation” is given by Günter Prockl (GP). The Literature is Textbook: Chapters 3, 17 and Reading List: No. 34, 35. The content focuses on SCM and the strategy hierarchy, supply chain design considerations, and supply chain strategic levers and decision-making components.

On 21st March, the session “14. Contemporary Issues in Logistics and SCM” concludes the course. The Literature for this session is Textbook: Chapter 15, 16, and the Faculty is Günter Prockl (GP). It addresses the latest challenges in logistics and SCM, provides a recap of the course, and answers “What’s in it for me?” by highlighting salient features of the course, key learning points, and managerial implications.
