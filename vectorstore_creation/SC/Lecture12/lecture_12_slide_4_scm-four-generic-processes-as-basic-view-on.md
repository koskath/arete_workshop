# Here is what the image in slide 4 of lecture 12 contains:

### 1. Order to Payment (Left and Right Sides)
The diagram shows two mirrored Order to Payment cycles positioned on the left and right edges:
- **Order Entry** initiates the process
- **Order Fulfilment** executes the order
- **Payment Flow** completes the cycle
- Red arrows indicate the flow direction moving from order entry through fulfillment to payment
- Blue arrows show reverse flows in the payment process

### 2. Idea to Market (Top Section)
The top horizontal flow represents product development and market introduction:
- **Research/Development** begins the process
- **Strategic Marketing** follows
- **Media/Salesforce Deployment** bridges to market readiness
- **Production Readiness** prepares for launch
- **Key Account** and **Sales Process** connect to business readiness
- Arrows flow from left to right across the **Costumer Market** axis

### 3. Costumer Relationship Management Process (Central Section)
The middle area contains operational processes:
- **Forecasting** initiates planning
- **Production/Distribution** handles manufacturing and logistics
- **Warehousing** manages inventory
- Processes are enclosed in a shaded region labeled **Vendors/Suppliers** on the left
- Flow moves horizontally through the operational chain

### 4. Controlling (Bottom Section)
The bottom shows management and control processes:
- **Plans/Budget** create financial frameworks
- Controlling processes monitor both left and right Order to Payment cycles
- Bidirectional arrows indicate feedback loops

## Integration Elements
- **Y-Chain Management** label appears on the left vertical axis
- **Costumer Market** forms the central horizontal axis
- **Adaptation and Evolution** spans the bottom, indicating continuous improvement
- Gray boxes labeled **Costumer** appear on both left and right sides, connecting the different process flows
